Best Restaurants in Hackney: 12 brilliant places I’d actually recommend

Hackney has no shortage of good places to eat. However, the best restaurants in Hackney are not just good. They feel exciting. They have atmosphere. They have point of view. Most importantly, they leave you wanting to come back.

That is what sets Hackney apart. Dinner here rarely feels generic. One evening might mean exceptional pasta in a beautiful room. Another could be bold Malaysian cooking, a polished tasting menu, smoky fire-led sharing plates or one of East London’s best canal-side tables.

If you are wondering where to eat in Hackney, this is my edit of the restaurants really worth knowing. These are the places I would genuinely recommend for date nights, catch-ups, long lunches and special dinners in East London.

Why Hackney is one of the best places to eat in London

Hackney’s food scene works because it still feels personal. Even the most stylish restaurants usually feel rooted in the neighbourhood. They are not all trying to look the same, sound the same or serve the same menu.

As a result, the best restaurants in Hackney offer real variety. Some are refined and quietly elegant. Others are lively, moody and full of energy. Some focus on beautiful produce and precise cooking. Others are all about bold flavours, warmth and character.

That mix is exactly why Hackney remains one of the most exciting areas in London for eating out.

12 best restaurants in Hackney right now

1. Tiella, Columbia Road

If you want one of the most exciting restaurant bookings in Hackney right now, start here. Tiella feels stylish, current and full of warmth. It has the kind of buzz that makes a dinner feel like a real event, yet it never feels overdone.

For me, this is one of the strongest Italian restaurants in Hackney because it gets the balance right. It feels romantic, lively and polished, while still being all about the pleasure of eating well.

Why go

Go for a date night, a celebratory dinner or whenever you want somewhere that feels like a proper occasion.

3. Café Cecilia, Hackney Road

Café Cecilia has become one of Hackney’s essential tables. It has an understated confidence that makes it feel timeless rather than flashy. The canal-side setting only adds to the appeal.

What I love about it is the ease. It feels refined, but never stiff. Cool, but not forced. It is one of those restaurants where the room, the mood and the food all work together beautifully.

Why go

Perfect for a stylish lunch, a refined dinner or one of those meals where you want everything to feel effortless.

4. Miga, Hackney

Miga is one of the most likeable restaurants in Hackney. It feels warm, characterful and full of flavour. In a borough where newer openings often take the spotlight, this is the kind of place that stands out because people genuinely want to return.

That staying power matters. Great restaurants are not just about hype. They are about how a place makes you feel, and Miga feels generous, memorable and easy to recommend.

Why go

A brilliant pick for a relaxed dinner that still feels special.

5. Angelina, Dalston

Angelina is one of Hackney’s most distinctive special occasion restaurants. It feels elegant, inventive and carefully considered from the moment you walk in.

This is somewhere to book when the dinner itself is the plan. It feels refined, but not cold. Creative, but still inviting. That is a difficult balance to get right, and Angelina does it beautifully.

Why go

Book it for birthdays, anniversaries or any evening where you want somewhere more elevated.

6. Casa Fofō, Clapton

Casa Fofō is one of the best restaurants in Hackney for a longer, slower dinner. It has the kind of precision and ambition that makes it ideal for a celebration, yet it still feels connected to the neighbourhood.

That is part of its charm. It feels serious, but never detached. Thoughtful, but still warm. If you are after a more elevated restaurant in Clapton, this is one of the strongest picks.

Why go

Best for a proper special occasion dinner in East London.

7. Oren, Dalston

Oren is one of those restaurants that is simply very easy to recommend. It is lively, welcoming and full of flavour, with the kind of menu that works especially well for sharing.

This is where I would send people who want a fun dinner in Hackney without losing quality. It has energy, but it is never chaotic. It feels stylish, yet still relaxed.

Why go

Ideal for date nights, group dinners and casual celebrations.

8. Sesta, Wilton Way

Sesta feels like a very current Hackney restaurant in the best sense. It is chef-led, stylish and produce-focused, but it still has a relaxed neighbourhood feel.

That makes it a strong choice for anyone who wants somewhere modern and considered without dipping into formality. It feels contemporary, but never try-hard.

Why go

A great choice when you want a polished dinner in a room that still feels easy-going.

9. Acme Fire Cult, Dalston

Not every great Hackney restaurant has to be candlelit and quietly elegant. Acme Fire Cult is proof of that. It is bold, smoky and full of personality, with a more playful feel than many of the borough’s polished dining rooms.

This is one of the best places in Hackney when you want dinner to feel social, energetic and a little more unexpected.

Why go

Come here for fire-led cooking, a lively atmosphere and something less conventional.

10. Café Kowloon, London Fields

Café Kowloon brings a more current, nightlife-leaning energy to Hackney’s food scene. It feels atmospheric, cool and slightly under the radar, which is part of the appeal.

This is not the place for a quiet, polished dinner. Instead, it is one to book when you want somewhere with edge, mood and a bit more buzz.

Why go

Perfect when you want dinner to blur into the rest of the night.

11. Behind, London Fields

If you are after a destination dinner in Hackney, Behind stands out. It offers a more immersive, occasion-led experience and brings serious fine dining weight to the area.

I like having it in this list because it shows how much range Hackney has now. It is not only about neighbourhood cool. It can also deliver one of the most high-end dining experiences in East London.

Why go

Book it when you want the food to be the main event.

12. Albers, De Beauvoir

Albers is one of the quieter names on this list, but it deserves a place. A strong Hackney restaurant guide should not only focus on the loudest openings. It should also include the restaurants that feel stylish, enjoyable and worth knowing.
