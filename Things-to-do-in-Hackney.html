Things To Do in Islington: The Ultimate Local Guide (2026)

Few London neighbourhoods combine culture, food, nightlife and history quite like Islington. Just north of the City, the borough stretches from the restaurant-lined streets of Angel to the leafy elegance of Highbury, the design studios of Clerkenwell and the buzzing cultural quarter around King’s Cross.

At first glance, Islington feels sophisticated and relaxed. Georgian townhouses line quiet streets, boutique shops fill hidden passages, and cafés spill onto pavements. But scratch beneath the surface and you’ll find one of London’s most dynamic cultural and culinary scenes.

Things To Do in Islington

Upper Street alone is packed with restaurants, cocktail bars, theatres and late-night venues. A short walk away, the Regent’s Canal runs through peaceful towpaths lined with waterside pubs and narrowboats. Markets like Camden Passage and Chapel Market add a vibrant local energy, while theatres such as Sadler’s Wells and Almeida Theatre attract world-class productions.

Food lovers come for the restaurants — from Michelin Guide favourites to hidden ramen bars and neighbourhood gastropubs. Nightlife fans head for nearby institutions like Fabric, Egg London, and Scala, while live music venues like Union Chapel, O2 Academy Islington, and Islington Assembly Hall keep the borough buzzing year-round.

What makes Islington special is its balance. It’s stylish but welcoming, historic yet constantly evolving. Locals love it for its parks, cafés and community feel. Visitors discover it for its restaurants, theatres and nightlife.

This ultimate guide from Islington Local Guide brings together over 100 of the best places and experiences across the borough — from restaurants and coffee shops to hidden cocktail bars, markets, cultural venues and scenic walks.

Whether you’re planning a weekend in North London or searching for hidden gems locals swear by, here are the best things to do in Islington in 2026.
